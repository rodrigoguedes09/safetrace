Understanding SafeTrace's blockchain compliance technology

What is SafeTrace?

SafeTrace is a comprehensive blockchain compliance platform that provides Know Your Transaction (KYT) analysis across multiple blockchain networks. Our technology helps businesses and individuals assess the risk profile of cryptocurrency transactions in real-time.

By analyzing transaction patterns, wallet interactions, and on-chain behavior, SafeTrace identifies potential risks associated with illicit activities, money laundering, sanctioned entities, and other compliance concerns.

How It Works

1

Transaction Input

You provide a transaction hash from any supported blockchain network. SafeTrace supports over 40 blockchain networks including Ethereum, Bitcoin, BNB Chain, Polygon, and more.

2

Graph Traversal Analysis

Our system uses advanced graph algorithms (Breadth-First Search) to trace the transaction across multiple hops. We analyze the flow of funds, identify involved addresses, and map the entire transaction network up to a configurable depth.

3

Risk Scoring

Each transaction is evaluated against multiple risk indicators including sanctioned addresses, known mixers/tumblers, exchange patterns, and suspicious transaction behaviors. Our multi-layered risk scoring model assigns a comprehensive risk score from 0-100.

4

Detailed Report

You receive a comprehensive analysis report with risk scores, identified patterns, flagged addresses, and recommendations. Reports can be viewed online or exported as PDF certificates for compliance documentation.

Risk Scoring Model

SafeTrace employs a sophisticated multi-factor risk scoring algorithm that evaluates transactions across several dimensions:

Low Risk (0-33)

Transactions showing normal patterns with verified counterparties, consistent with legitimate business activities. No suspicious indicators detected.

Medium Risk (34-66)

Transactions with some concerning patterns such as interaction with high-volume exchanges, privacy-focused services, or unusual transaction structures. Requires additional review.

High Risk (67-100)

Transactions involving sanctioned addresses, known mixers/tumblers, darknet markets, ransomware addresses, or other high-risk entities. Immediate attention and enhanced due diligence recommended.
